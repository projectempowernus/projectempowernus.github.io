Who are We?

Founded in January 2022, Project Empower is a Local Community Involvement Project (LCIP) under NUS Medical Society that aims to empower caregivers. We hope to advance society’s caregiving readiness and raise awareness on the prevalence of caregiver burnout. To do so, we are adopting a multipronged approach, one that involves the collaboration of multiple stakeholders, in order to meet our goals.

What do we do?

In summary, we are targeting caregivers by:

  1. Providing ad hoc respite-care services (e.g. helping them purchase groceries, cleaning their homes, offer basic caregiving training, help them understand what to expect throughout the entire caregiver journey, trauma services etc)
  2. Public health & relevant research
  3. Raisingawareness on the importance of caregiver burnout through several public initiatives, such as a podcast (we are in the midst of launching it, so stay tuned!)
